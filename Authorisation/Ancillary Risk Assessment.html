Rating elements of risk controls:
  • Strong: Indicates that the firm’s risk management systems are generally satisfactory in relation to its size, complexity and risk profile. While the firm may have some minor risk management weakness, these problems have been recognized and are being addressed. Overall, board and senior management oversight, policies and limits, risk-monitoring procedures, reports, and management information systems are considered effective. Risks are generally being controlled in a manner that does not require more than normal supervisory attention.
  • Acceptable: Indicates risk management systems meet what is considered necessary. Risk management practices are less than satisfactory given the nature of the firm’s activities. The capabilities of management or the board of directors may be insufficient for the type, size or condition of the firm. Problems and significant risks may be inadequately identified, measured, monitored or controlled. Such weaknesses if not addressed could become problematic for the institution.
  • Needs improvement: Indicates risk management systems that are deficient in important ways and, therefore, are a cause for urgent supervisory attention. Problems and risk exposure are significant and are inadequately identified, measured, monitored or controlled and require immediate action by the board and management. The internal control system may be lacking in important respects, particularly as indicated by continued control exceptions or by the failure to adhere to written policies and procedures. The deficiencies associated in these systems could have adverse effects on the safety and soundness of the firm or could lead to a material mis-statement of its financial statements if corrective actions are not taken. Replacing or strengthening of management or the board may be necessary.
  • Deficient: Indicates risk management systems which threaten the viability of the firm. Problems and risk exposure are significant and the management and board have been unable to identify measure, monitor or control the risk. Internal controls are critically deficient and the institution may be unable to comply with key regulatory requirements. Replacing or strengthening management is necessary.
Rating elements of inherent risk:
  • Low Inherent Risk: Low inherent risk exists when there is a lower than average probability of a material loss due to exposure to, and uncertainty arising from, current and potential future events.
  • Moderate Low Inherent Risk: Moderate low inherent risk exists when there is an average probability of a material loss due to exposure to, and uncertainty arising from, current and potential future events.
  • Moderate High Inherent Risk: Moderate inherent risk exists when there is an above average probability of a material loss due to exposure to, and uncertainty arising from, current and potential future events.
  • High Inherent Risk: High inherent risk exists when there is a higher than above average probability of a material loss due to exposure to, and uncertainty arising from, current and potential future events.
Rating elements of inherent risk:
  • Low: Well Controlled
  • Medium-Low: Controlled
  • Medium-High: Needs Improvement
  • High: Unacceptable
