PREAMBLE

This agreement (“the Agreement”) is entered into by and between:

Astana Financial Services Authority (“AFSA”), a legal entity established pursuant to article 12 of the Constitutional Statue of the Republic of Kazakhstan on the Astana International Financial Centre, Constitutional Statute No. 438-V of 7 December 2015 (“the Constitutional Statute”) and having its principal place of business located at 55 Mangilik El, building 15, Astana, Republic of Kazakhstan, represented by Acting Chief Executive Officer, Mr Mukhtar Bubeyev, acting on the basis of the decision of the Board of Directors of the Astana Financial Services Authority No.12 dated 15 June 2018; and

[MainCompanyName] (“the Company”), a legal entity established pursuant to the legislation of [details] and having its principal place of business located at [*TODO], represented by [*TODO], [*TODO], acting pursuant to [*TODO];

each being referred to individually as a “Party” or as defined above and collectively as “the Parties”.



Whereas:

A.

The Company seeks to be admitted as a Recognised Non-AIFC Member (“a RNAM”) of an Authorised Market Institution (“an AMI”) within the meaning of the AIFC Authorised Market Institution Rules, AIFC Rules No. FR0002 of 2017 (“the AMI Rules”); AND

B.

The AFSA undertakes, subject to the provisions set out in rule 91 of the AIFC Financial Services Framework Regulations, AIFC Regulations No. 18 of 2017 (“the FSFR”) being satisfied, to make an order declaring the Company to be a RNAM; AND

C.

The Company agrees to submit to and comply with regulation by the AFSA on the terms set out in this Agreement in satisfaction of rule 91(3)(e) of the FSFR and as a necessary condition precedent and continuing condition of being declared by the AFSA to be a RNAM.

The Guarantees
Making the Guarantees

5.

The Company undertakes and guarantees that (“the Guarantees”):

a.

the Company will cooperate with the AFSA in accordance with the terms of clause 6 below (“the Cooperation Guarantee”);

b.

the Company will subject itself to such parts of the legal and regulatory framework administered by the AFSA as the AFSA may require in accordance with the terms of clause 8 below (“the Submission Guarantee”).

6.

The AFSA shall be entitled to amend the Prescribed Rules in any way whatsoever, including by adding additional rules and/or regulations, by removing rules and/or regulations, and/or by amending the content of the rules and/or regulations. The Company acknowledges that it is solely responsible for monitoring the amendments introduced to the Prescribed Rules on the official website of the AFSA. For avoidance of doubt Company is responsible to initiate execution of agreement or any other instrument required to give effect to any such amendments.




4


The Cooperation Guarantee

7.

The terms of the Cooperation Guarantee are:

a.

the Company shall provide such assistance to the AFSA as the AFSA shall reasonably require for the purposes of the AFSA exercising its power under article 12 of the Constitutional Statute;

b.

the Company shall co-operate with and comply with instructions and orders issued by the AIFC Court and/or issued or procured by the AFSA;

c.

the Company shall notify the AFSA within seven (7) Business Days of any changes in circumstances which render inaccurate any of the details set out in this Agreement;

d.

the Company shall notify the AFSA within seven (7) Business Days of any breach by the Company of the obligations set out in this Agreement, including, for the avoidance of doubt, any breach of the Warranties and Guarantees, and any breaches of the Prescribed Rules.


The Submission Guarantee

8.

The terms of the Submission Guarantee are:

a.

the Company shall comply with and be bound by the AIFC rules and regulations set out in Schedule 1 to the Agreement (“the Prescribed Rules”). Each of the Prescribed Rules shall apply to the Company as if the Company were a member of the class of entities in respect of whom each of the Prescribed Rules apply;

b.

the Company shall comply with MR 1.2.2 of the AIX Rules;

c.

the Company shall comply with any rules made from time to time by the AFSA pursuant to regulation 91(4) of the FSFR prescribing ongoing criteria which the Company must satisfy in order to continue to be a RNAM.



















5

RNAM Status
Order of RNAM Status

9.

In consideration of the Company entering into this Agreement and the Warranties and Guarantees contained herein, the AFSA shall make an order pursuant to regulation 91(1) of the FSFR (“an RNAM Order”) that the Company is to be a Recognised Non-AIFC Member (“RNAM”), provided that the AFSA is satisfied that the other conditions set out in regulation 91 of the FSFR are satisfied

10.

For the avoidance of doubt:

a.

nothing in this Agreement shall oblige or compel the AFSA to make an RNAM Order in circumstances where the AFSA is not satisfied that any and all of the conditions set out in regulation 91 of the FSFR are satisfied.

b.

the determination as to whether the conditions set out in regulation 91 of the FSFR are satisfied shall be made at the AFSA’s sole discretion.


Revocation of RNAM Status

11.

The AFSA shall be entitled to revoke or vary any RNAM Order made in respect of the Company if the AFSA determines, at its sole discretion, that it is necessary or desirable to do so in pursuit of the AFSA’s Regulatory Objectives, as defined in regulation 7(3) of the FSFR, with the Company being treated as if it were a Centre Participant for the purposes of regulation 7(3) of the FSFR.


Consequences Of Breach Of The Warranties ANd/OR Guarantees

12.

Where, in the determination of the ASFA at its sole discretion, there has been any breach by the Company of the obligations set out in this Agreement, including, for the avoidance of doubt, any breach of the Warranties and Guarantees, and any breaches of the Prescribed Rules:

a.

the AFSA shall be entitled to revoke or vary any RNAM Order made in respect of the Company;

b.

the Company shall comply with any orders or instructions imposing a penalty on the Company in accordance with any applicable rules and/or regulations adopted by the AFSA in accordance with its powers under article 12(3) of the Constitutional Statute.

Termination

19.

This Agreement shall come into effect as at the date first written above.

a.

The Notice of Termination shall specify a Date of Termination upon which this Agreement shall come to an end. The Date of Termination shall be at least 14 days after the date of deemed service of the Notice of Termination.

20.

Termination of this Agreement shall not absolve the Company from its obligations and responsibilities under the terms of the Warranties and Guarantees in respect of any acts of events prior to the Date of Termination.

21.

The following provisions of this Agreement shall survive the termination of this Agreement: clauses 1, 2, 7, 8, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 17, , 20, 21, 22, 2324, 25, and all other provisions of this Agreement that by their nature extend beyond the termination of this Agreement.

Confidentiality

26.

The Parties shall keep all Confidential Information confidential and shall not: (a) use such Confidential Information except for the purpose of exercising or performing its rights and obligations under or in connection with this Agreement (the “Permitted Purpose”); or (b) disclose such Confidential Information in whole or in part to any third party, except as expressly permitted herein.

27.

A Party may disclose Confidential Information to those of its Representatives who need to know such Confidential Information for the Permitted Purpose, provided that: (a) it informs such Representatives of the confidential nature of the Confidential Information before disclosure; and (b) it procures that its Representatives shall, in relation to any Confidential Information disclosed to them, comply with the confidentiality obligations set out in this Agreement as if they were a party to this Agreement, and at all times shall be liable for the failure of any Representatives to comply with the obligations set out in this clause 27.

28.

A Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent such Confidential Information is required to be disclosed by law, by any governmental or other regulatory authority or by a court or other authority of competent jurisdiction provided that, to the extent permitted by the relevant law or relevant body, the such Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of any such request so that the other Party may undertake measures for the protection of the Confidential Information and the first Party shall consult with the other Party in order to agree the timing and content of the disclosure and shall fully co-operate with the other Party if it elects to challenge the validity of the requirement to disclose to the extent it is legally permitted to do so, it gives the other Party as much notice of such disclosure as possible and, where notice of disclosure is not prohibited and is given in accordance with this Clause 28, it takes into account the reasonable requests of the other Party in relation to the content of such disclosure.

29.

The following shall not be considered Confidential information: (a) information that is or becomes generally available to the public (other than as a result of its disclosure by the receiving Party or its Representatives in breach the confidentiality provisions hereof; (b) information that was available to the receiving Party on a non-confidential basis before disclosure by the disclosing Party; (c) information that was, is or becomes available to the receiving Party on a non-confidential basis from a person who, to the receiving Party's knowledge, is not bound by a confidentiality agreement with the disclosing Party or otherwise prohibited from disclosing the information to the receiving Party.

30.

Confidentiality provisions hereof shall remain in force and effect within the direction of this Agreement and for three (3) years following its termination.
