In-Principle Approval to the Fintech Lab
 
1. General Provisions
1.1. This In-Principle Acceptance to the Fintech Lab is given by the AFSA under sections 32 and 33 of the AIFC Financial Services Framework Regulations ("FSFR") and section 1.4 of AIFC General Rules ("GEN").
 
2. Permited FinTech Activities
2.1. In accordance with section 30(1) of FSFR, section 1.4 of GEN and section 2.4.1 of AIFC Financial Technology Rules ("FINTECH"), the AFSA has decided to grant [PsApplicantFullName] (the "Firm") a Licence to carry on the following FinTech Lab Activities ("FinTech Licence"):
 
 
2.2. Granting the FinTech Licence is subject to the Pre-Conditions set out bellow being fulfilled to the AFSA's satisfaction within the specified deadlines below.
 
3. Pre-Conditions
3.1. The Firm shall submit or cause to be submitted to the AFSA:
 
1. Within one month from the date of receiving this letter:
a) written confirmation that there have been no material changes in the Firm’s circumstances since the submission of its application for a FinTech Licence; and
b) written confirmation of the Firm’s acceptance of the Waiver Conditions prescribed below;
 
2. Within three months from the date of receiving this letter:
a) Certificate of Incorporation or Registration issued by Registrar of Companies of the AFSA for an entity to which the FinTech Licence will be granted.
 
Note:  If the Firm fails to fulfil the Pre-Conditions within the specified deadline the AFSA may revoke this In-Principle Acceptance and consider the application as withdrawn by the Firm without prior notice. The AFSA at its discretion may reinstate this In-Principle Acceptance by a reasonable request from the Firm.

5. Licence Conditions
 
The AFSA proposes to impose the following requirements** in accordance with sections 35(1)(a), 35(2)(d), 98 (a), 99 and 100 of the FSFR and section 2.6 of FINTECH to be fulfilled before and during providing service(s) to Clients in the course of carrying on permitted FinTech Lab Activities ("Live Environment").
 
General Requirements (applicable to all FinTech Lab Participants)
 
The Firm shall:
(1) Act only for the purposes of or in connection with carrying on permitted FinTech Lab Activities;
(2) Obtain AFSA’s approval before commencing operations in Live Environment and providing services to Clients;
(3) Develop and implement adequate policies and procedures for (without limitation) AML/FT, risk and compliance management arrangements and comply with all IT system requirements (excluding IT audit) before commencing operations in Live Environment;
(4) Maintain the developed policies and procedures during Live Environment to ensure the continuous operation with respect to any changes;
(5) Conduct its activities in the FinTech Lab in good faith and accordance with applicable Acting Law of the AIFC, licence conditions and control boundaries prescribed in this document, and disclose to the AFSA immediately as soon as it becomes aware of:
a. any breach, or likely breach of licensing conditions and boundaries as well as of a provision of the AIFC legislation; or
b. a failure, or likely failure, to comply with any obligation to which the Firm is subject under such legislation;
(6) In the event of any irregularities (including breaches or failures), undertake all necessary steps to rectify them promptly and in collaboration with the AFSA, with the view to ensure that the operation of the Firm will continue to proceed smoothly;
(7) Conduct proper due diligence and comply with legislation of the jurisdiction where the Firm wants to conduct permitted FinTech Activities, engage Clients and conduct Financial Promotion and marketing;
(8) Make appropriate disclosures to its Clients, including but not limited to, risks, clarity on being licenced with conditions and limitations under FinTech Lab; contractual relationships with other financial institutions, terms and conditions that may affect the Client or provision of services; details on fees, costs and other charges; exit strategy in event of operational disruption or cessation of activities; and as prescribed by the AFSA.
(9) Ensure that records protected from loss, destruction, falsification, unauthorized access and release, in accordance with legislative, regulatory, contractual and business requirements;
(10) Provide the AFSA Fintech Office access to the platform for account reconciliation and for observation the activities of the Firm to ensure compliance with the control boundaries for testing at any time upon request from the AFSA;
(11) Clearly define the information security-related roles and responsibilities of the Board, senior management, governing bodies and individuals with responsibility for decision-making, approval, oversight, operations and other information security functions;
(12) Implement and use standardized conformance testing procedures in line with internationally accepted standards to test the viability and effectiveness of information technology systems, including system resilience and security. The Applicant, before commencing operation to Live Environment, must:
a. Ensure that the following tests, at a minimum, are completed at satisfactory level:
1. Development/Configuration/Customization;
2. Satisfactory completing stress and performance testing;
3. Successful testing of all interfaces and related systems required for operation;
b. Submit the "IT system authorisation questionnaire" provided by the AFSA. The IT system authorisation questionnaire enables firms to confirm their system conform with good industry practice and has appropriate systems in place which are fit for purpose to conduct live environment IT activities within the AFSA FinTech Lab;
(13) Maintain an information security capability and policy framework commensurate with the size and extent of threats to its information assets, and which enables the continued sound operation of the entity and with respect to changes in vulnerabilities and threats, including those resulting from changes to information assets or its business environment;
(14) Classify its information assets, including those managed by related parties and third parties, by criticality and sensitivity. This classification must reflect the degree to which an information security incident affecting an information asset has the potential to affect (financially or non-financially) clients, beneficiaries, AFSA, and other customers;
(15) Implement robust mechanisms in place to detect and respond to information security incidents in a timely manner (information security response plans);
(16) Implement controls to protect its information assets commensurate with the criticality and sensitivity of those information assets, and undertake systematic testing and assurance regarding the design and operational effectiveness of those controls by independent internal function e.g. Internal Audit Function;
(17) Within one year after start of the operations in Live Environment, conduct IT Audit by independent third-party (Certified Information Security Auditor (CISA) or other equivalent certification holder) to test the design and operational effectiveness of the implemented controls;
(18) Where information assets are managed by a related party or third party, assess the information security capability of that party, commensurate with the potential consequences of an information security incident affecting those assets;
(19) Notify AFSA as soon as possible and, in any case, no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of an information security incident, which has materially affected, or had the potential to materially affect (financially or non-financially) the entity or the interests of clients, beneficiaries and other customers;
(20) Appoint Chief Information Officer, an individual as responsible for ongoing IT operations, maintenance and security oversight to ensure that its information technology systems are reliable and adequately protected from external attack or incident;
(21) Appoint Data Protection Individual (Data Controller or Legal Person), an individual as responsible for safeguarding personal data who controls and is responsible for the keeping and use of personal information according to AIFC Data Protection Rules;
(22) Report to AFSA FinTech Office on carrying on FinTech Lab Activities on a monthly basis;
(23) Obtain prior approval from the AFSA on fees, commissions and any other charges imposed on clients by the Firm before commencing to Live Environment ("Approved Fee Structure"). Thereafter, provide periodical post-notification reports to the AFSA of any changes, including increase or decrease in sizes of fees, commissions and any other charges, or modifications and notify in at least two (2) business days in advance the AFSA of any structural changes to the Approved Fee Structure. The AFSA reserves the right to reject the changes even after the implementation (on reasonable grounds) by giving at least five (5) calendar days prior notice.
(24) Notify AFSA FinTech Office on opening any bank accounts;
(25) In bank account opening agreements, include the right of AFSA to request information about that account without consent of the Firm - account owner.
