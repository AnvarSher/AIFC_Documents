5. Licence conditions
The AFSA proposes to impose the following conditions/restrictions/requirements* in the Licence under sections 35(1)(a), 35(2)(d), 98 (a) of the FSFR and section 2.6 of FINTECH:
5.1. General conditions
The firm must:
(1) Within the FinTech Lab act only for the purposes of carrying out permitted FinTech Lab Activities;
(2) Obtain the AFSA’s approval before commencing operations in a live environment or providing products or services to Clients;
(3) Develop and implement adequate policies and procedures for (without limitation) AML/FT, risk and compliance management arrangements and comply with all IT system requirements (excluding IT audit) before commencing operations in Live Environment;
(4) Maintain the developed policies and procedures during Live Environment to ensure the continuous operation with respect to any changes;
(5) Carry out its activities in the FinTech Lab in accordance with applicable Acting Law of the AIFC, Licence conditions and boundary conditions prescribed in this document or other document provided by the AFSA, and inform the AFSA immediately if it becomes aware of any breach, or likely breach, of a Licence condition, the boundary conditions or any other requirement applying to the firm under the Acting Law of the AIFC.
(6) Undertake all necessary steps to rectify breaches and other irregularities promptly and in collaboration with the AFSA, with a view to ensure that the operation of the firm will continue to proceed smoothly;
(7) Conduct proper due diligence and comply with the legislation of the jurisdiction where the Firm wants to conduct its permitted FinTech Lab Activities, engage with Clients, and conduct financial promotion and marketing;
(8) Make appropriate disclosures to its Clients, including, for example, in relation to the following: risks; limitations under its Licence conditions; contractual relationships with other financial institutions; terms and conditions that may affect the Clients or provision of products or services; details of fees, costs and other charges; exit strategy in the event of operational disruption or cessation of activities; other matters required by the AFSA.
(9) Ensure that its records are protected from loss, destruction, falsification, and unauthorised access and release, in accordance with legislative, regulatory, contractual and business requirements;
(10) Provide the AFSA FinTech Office with access to the platform for account reconciliation and observation of the activities of the firm to ensure compliance with the control boundaries at AFSA request;
(11) Clearly define the information security-related roles and responsibilities of the Board, senior management, governing bodies, and individuals with responsibility for decision-making, approval, oversight, operations and other information security functions within the firm;
(12) Implement and use standardized conformance testing procedures in line with internationally accepted standards to test the viability and effectiveness of information technology systems, including system resilience and security. The Firm before commencing operations in Live Environment must:
a. Ensure that the following tests, at a minimum, are completed at satisfactory level:
i. Development/Configuration/Customization;
ii. Satisfactory completing stress and performance testing;
iii. Successful testing of all interfaces and related systems required for operation;
b. Submit the “IT system authorisation questionnaire” provided by the AFSA. The IT system authorisation questionnaire enables firms to confirm their system conform with good industry practice and has appropriate systems in place which are fit for purpose to conduct live environment IT activities within the AFSA FinTech Lab;
(13) Maintain an information security capability and policy framework commensurate with the size and extent of threats to its information assets, and that enables the continued sound operation of the firm in the case of vulnerabilities and threats, including those resulting from changes to information assets or its business environment;
(14) Classify its information assets, including those managed by related parties and third parties, by criticality and sensitivity and, in particular, in a way that reflects the degree to which an information security incident affecting an information asset has the potential to affect (financially or non-financially) Clients, beneficiaries, the AFSA, and other customers;
(15) Implement robust mechanisms to detect and respond to information security incidents in a timely manner (i.e. information security response plans);
(16) Implement controls to protect its information assets commensurate with their criticality and sensitivity, and undertake systematic testing and assurance regarding the design and operational effectiveness of those controls by independent internal function e.g. internal audit function;
(17) Within one year after the start of the live test, arrange for an IT Audit to be completed by an independent third-party (a Certified Information Security Auditor (CISA) or other equivalent certification holder) to test the design and operational effectiveness of the implemented controls to protect its information assets;
(18) If information assets are managed by a related party or third party, assess the information security capability of that party, commensurate with the potential consequences of an information security incident affecting those assets;
(19) Notify the AFSA as soon as possible and, in any case, no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of an information security incident that has materially affected, or had the potential to materially affect, (financially or non-financially) the entity or the interests of Clients, beneficiaries, the ASFA, and other customers;
(20) Appoint an individual who, as its Chief Information Officer, is responsible for ongoing IT operations, maintenance and security oversight to ensure that its information technology systems are reliable and adequately protected from external attack or incident;
(21) Appoint an individual who is responsible for protection of personal data and compliance of the firm with data protection requirements;
(22) Report, on a monthly basis, to the AFSA FinTech Office on the carrying out of permitted FinTech Activities;
(23) Before carrying out activities in a live environment, obtain the prior written approval of the AFSA for the type and amount of fees and other payments that it is to charge its Clients (Client fees). Thereafter, obtain the prior written approval of the AFSA for any change in the Client fees. The Client fee can be decreased or reduced by the firm without obtaining the AFSA’s approval. Should the Client fee be decreased or reduced – the firm must notify in written the AFSA on such a change to its Client fees within two (2) business days upon its decision on decrease or reduction.
(24) Notify the AFSA FinTech Office within one month of the opening by the firm of an account in a financial institution, including a bank;
(25) Include, in each bank account opening agreement, authority for the AFSA to request any information about the account without the consent of the firm or account owner.
5.2. Tailored Conditions
(1) Hold client’s funds in segregated Client Money Account at the third-party account provider that is a Bank or a Regulated Financial Institution which is authorised to accept or take Deposits;
(2) Provide a bank guarantee, insurance, a deposit in an escrow account at the third-party account provider that is a Bank or a Regulated Financial Institution which is authorised to accept or take Deposits, or other form of equally satisfactory security in an amount which is not less than the clients’ funds that will be kept by the company.
